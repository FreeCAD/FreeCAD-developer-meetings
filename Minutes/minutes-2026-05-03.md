# FreeCAD Developer Meeting

3 May 2026

Jit.si Online Meeting

---

## Migration to Nextcloud

The discussion began with an update on the migration to Nextcloud, a self-hosted, open-source platform designed to replace services like Google Cloud for project files.

*   **Instance:** Currently online at https://cloud.freecad.org
*   **Purpose and Scope:** Nextcloud is intended for shared project files and internal documentation, moving away from third-party cloud solutions. This includes migrating existing services, such as the FreeCAD calendar, which has now been moved to the Nextcloud calendar.
*   **Access and Infrastructure:** Users needing an account on the Nextcloud instance are advised to contact a maintainer. Regarding infrastructure concerns, it was clarified that the Nextcloud instance runs on its own droplet, separate from the wiki's infrastructure, which has experienced past issues. The FreeCAD.org domain is managed with redundancy, with four administrators having access to assets and droplets.
*   **User Experience:** The calendar at https://freecad.org/events.php was migrated, and is designed to be seamless for users. "It looks identical. It shouldn't look any different to you, but under the hood, it is a different calendar."

## FreeCAD Release Process

A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to discussing improvements to the FreeCAD release process:

*   **Release Cadence and LTS:** The proposal suggests three releases per year: one Long-Term Support (LTS) release and two regular releases. The LTS release would focus on stability and bug fixing, while the regular releases might have a less stringent feature freeze. This aims to provide more frequent stable versions and reduce reliance on development branches.
*   **Defining "Blocker" Issues:** There was discussion around the definition of "blocker" issues for releases. Some argued for a stricter definition, focusing only on issues that corrupt data, while others felt that significant regressions or broken functionality should also be considered blockers.

## Continued AI Policy Discussion

The meeting then moved on to continuing the discussion about a proposed No-AI policy introduced in the previous meeting.

  *   **Concerns:** The primary concerns raised were the potential for "AI slop" in Pull Requests (PRs), the difficulty in identifying AI-generated code, potential copyright and licensing issues, and the impact on the development workflow and maintainer time. Yorik, Frank, and others expressed a strong preference for a "no AI" policy due to these risks.
  *   **Counterarguments and Proposals:** Kacper and others argued against a strict "no AI" policy, emphasizing that it would alienate good contributors who use AI tools ethically. They suggested a more nuanced approach focusing on disclosure of AI usage, vetting contributors, and having a policy that values human contribution and understanding.
  *   **Risks on Both Sides:** One group argued that the risks of allowing AI are high and so the project should default to a policy that "closes the door." They suggested that it could be re-opened at some future time if the situation changes and the concerns idenfied are addressed. The other group pointed out that the loss of developers alienated by the hard-line policy would not be recoverable, and that once gone, those developers will not return, so closing the door is not a "safe" option at all.
  *   **Next Steps:** Pieter volunteered to draft a proposal that incorporates Kacper's ideas and addresses ethical and copyright concerns, to be discussed at the next meeting.
